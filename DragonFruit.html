What is Dragon Fruit?

Dragon fruit, also known as pitaya fruit, is a tropical, cactus-based fruit, with a unique appearance and crunchy texture. Its appearance draws its name from a fire-breathing dragon. Its rich source of antioxidants plays a vital role in reducing your risks of cancer and diabetes. Make it a point to add this superfruit to your everyday diet.

What does it taste like?

Dragon Fruit tastes like a mixture of kiwi and pear. The very first time you cut open this fruit, it may seem like an Oreo smoothie because of its white pulp and tiny black seeds. This tropical fruit has loads of health benefits and It is usually enjoyed by the Vietnamese at the end of a meal as a great dessert for everyone in the family.

Dragon Fruit's Journey from Mexico to Worldwide Fame

Originally native to Mexico, dragon fruit, or red pitaya, has become a global sensation, finding cultivation in diverse regions such as Vietnam, Taiwan, Southern China, Israel, and more recently in Thailand, Australia, the United States, and Malaysia. Renowned for its striking purple-red hue and rich antioxidant content, dragon fruit has captivated the attention of the world.

Dragon Fruit is easy to enjoy

Cutting a dragon fruit is a simple process, and here’s how to do it:

  1. Choose a Ripe Dragon Fruit: Pick a fruit with vibrant, evenly colored skin and a slight give when gently pressed, indicating ripeness.
  2. Wash and Prepare: Rinse the fruit under cold water and scrub it gently. Use a knife to cut off the crown (the green spiky part) and the base, creating stable, flat ends.
  3. Cut in Half: Make a lengthwise cut down the middle of the fruit, resulting in two equal halves.
  4. Scoop or Slice: you can use a spoon to scoop out the flesh, however it’s best to slice it into bite-sized pieces and then peel the skin away from each piece.
